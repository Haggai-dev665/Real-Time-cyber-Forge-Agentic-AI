# CyberForge — Explainer Video Voiceover Script

A page-by-page narration that follows the **sidebar order, top to bottom** (Intelligence →
Model Inference → Agent System → HTTP History → Security Operations → Reports). Read one
block per screen while you demonstrate that page.

## How to use this

- **Pace:** ~145–155 words per minute, calm and confident. Each block is ~20–40 seconds —
  matched to a short live demo of that page.
- **Recording the voiceover:** record your screen demo first, then narrate over it. In
  **Canva**, open the video, click **Record yourself / Record voiceover**, narrate each
  block, then drag the audio along the timeline to line up with the matching screen. (You
  can also record audio separately and drag it in via **Uploads**.)
- **Cues:** `[DEMO: …]` tells you what to click/show while the matching `VO:` line is read.
- **Tone:** you're a guide, not a salesperson. Let the live demo prove the claims.

---

## 0 · Cold open  `[DEMO: loading logo animates → lands on Threat Overview]`

**VO:** "This is CyberForge — an AI‑powered security console that runs entirely on your own
machine. In the next few minutes I'll walk you through it screen by screen, exactly in the
order they appear down the left‑hand sidebar — from your live threat dashboard, through the
AI agent that does the thinking, to the reports it writes for you. One thing to keep in mind
throughout: everything you'll see runs locally. Your data never leaves this device."

`[DEMO: hover the sidebar so it expands from icons to labels]`

**VO:** "On the left is the navigation rail. It sits as a slim strip of icons and expands
when I hover over it. It's grouped into sections — Intelligence, Model Inference, Agent
System, HTTP History, Security Operations, and Reports. We'll take every page in turn, from
the very first to the last."

## SECTION 1 · INTELLIGENCE

### 1 · Threat Overview  `[DEMO: the dashboard, point to the KPI numbers + panels]`

**VO:** "First is Threat Overview — the home screen, and your situational dashboard. At a
glance it shows the total number of threats CyberForge is tracking, how many are critical
priority‑zero and high priority‑one alerts, and your overall security posture. These numbers
are live — they come straight from the agents continuously analysing the sites you visit.
Think of this as mission control: the first place you look to know whether everything is calm,
or something needs your attention."

### 2 · Threat Globe  `[DEMO: spin the globe, point to live arcs + the side panels]`

**VO:** "Next, the Threat Globe. This is a real, interactive 3‑D map of the planet — and
those moving arcs and pulses are genuine cyber‑attacks happening around the world right now,
pulled live from global threat‑intelligence feeds. On the side you can see the live attack
stream, the countries the most attacks are coming from, and the main methods being used. It's
a real‑time weather map for the internet — and as it watches, CyberForge quietly files what it
learns into its own memory."

### 3 · Sandbox  `[DEMO: submit a suspicious URL/file, show behaviour timeline + IOCs + ATT&CK]`

**VO:** "The Sandbox is a safe room. When you have a suspicious link or file, you can
'detonate' it here — open it inside an isolated chamber where it can't touch your real system
— and watch exactly what it tries to do. The behaviour timeline records every move it makes,
the indicators panel lists the tell‑tale signs of compromise, and the MITRE ATT&CK panel maps
those moves to the known playbook real attackers use. It's how you understand a threat without
ever putting yourself at risk."

### 4 · Browser Intelligence  `[DEMO: the domain graph, HTTP history, intercept queue]`

**VO:** "Browser Intelligence shows the web through CyberForge's eyes. The domain graph draws
out the hidden relationships between the sites and servers your browsing actually touches —
who talks to whom behind the scenes. Beside it you can see captured HTTP history, your active
browser sessions, and an intercept queue where anything questionable is held for a closer
look. It turns invisible background web traffic into a picture you can actually read."

### 5 · AI Assistance  `[DEMO: ask a question, e.g. "what were my riskiest sites this week?"]`

**VO:** "AI Assistance is where you simply talk to the system. It's powered by an advanced
reasoning model — DeepSeek — and, crucially, it's grounded in CyberForge's own memory, so it
actually knows what's happened on your machine. Ask it something like 'is this link safe?' or
'what were my riskiest sites this week?' and it answers in plain English, citing the real scans
and threats it has seen. You can run several conversations, start new ones, and revisit old
ones — like having a security analyst on call who never forgets a detail."

### 6 · Security Functions  `[DEMO: block a site, show it in Blocked list + response log]`

**VO:** "Security Functions is where you take action — and it's real. Blocked sites are
genuinely prevented from opening on your computer; protected sites are routed for safer access;
and allowed sites are the ones you've marked as trusted. Every response the system takes is
written to the response log, and the detection engines at the bottom show the machine‑learning
models powering it all. With a single click here, a dangerous website simply stops working —
no technical knowledge required."

### 7 · Signal Pipeline  `[DEMO: watch events streaming through the live flow]`

**VO:** "Signal Pipeline is the engine room. Every security signal — every page visited,
scanned, scored and acted on — flows through here in real time, stage by stage. It's a live
view of the system's nervous system, turning raw events into the alerts and insights you see
everywhere else. You never have to watch it, but it shows that the protection is always
working — not only when you're looking."

## SECTION 2 · MODEL INFERENCE

### 8 · Model Inference  `[DEMO: active models, live throughput graph]`

**VO:** "Under Model Inference you can see the artificial intelligence itself at work. This
screen lists the active models that classify links, flag dangerous domains and extract threat
indicators, along with their live throughput — how much they're analysing each second. It's
the proof behind the promise: CyberForge isn't matching simple keyword lists, it's running real
machine‑learning models that make a genuine judgement about every site."

---

## SECTION 3 · AGENT SYSTEM

### 9 · Agent Core  `[DEMO: type a URL into the terminal, watch it reason, show Block/Protect/Allow]`

**VO:** "Agent Core is the brain of the operation. CyberForge doesn't just follow fixed rules
— it runs an autonomous agent that reasons through problems step by step. On the left you can
see its capabilities and the resources it's using; in the centre, its reasoning console streams
its thinking out loud as it investigates. And right here, I can talk to it directly in the
built‑in terminal — hand it a website, ask it to investigate, then tell it to block, protect
or allow what it finds. This is agentic AI: software that decides and acts, not just software
that displays."

### 10 · Threat Analyzer  `[DEMO: the analyzed-sites list filling in, risk scores]`

**VO:** "Threat Analyzer works quietly in the background, and this is where its results live.
It takes your entire browsing history and methodically scans every site, one by one, scoring
each for risk with the machine‑learning engines. The list fills in as it goes, with the riskiest
results rising to the top. You never have to ask it to check anything — by the time you arrive,
it has already been through everywhere you've been."

### 11 · Scheduled  `[DEMO: scheduled jobs list, "up next", recent runs]`

**VO:** "Scheduled is the automation calendar. CyberForge can run tasks on a timetable —
regular history sweeps, intelligence refreshes, routine reports — so protection keeps happening
without you lifting a finger. Here you can see the jobs that are active, what's coming up next,
and a log of recent runs. Set it once, and it keeps watch on a schedule."

### 12 · Memory  `[DEMO: the memory graph, filter Episodic / Semantic / Procedural]`

**VO:** "Memory is what makes CyberForge feel like it truly knows you. Every scan, every threat,
every report and every action is stored as a memory — locally, on your device. This graph shows
how those memories connect to one another, and the list shows the most recent ones, sorted into
types: things it experienced, things it learned, and things it did. This is the very same memory
the AI assistant reads from — it's why the system grows smarter about your specific digital life
over time."

## SECTION 4 · HTTP HISTORY

### 13 · Browser History  `[DEMO: search/filter the history, scan one row on the spot]`

**VO:** "Browser History is the complete record of where your browsers have been — gathered
automatically from every browser on your machine, even ones that are closed, read straight from
your computer. You can search and filter it, and scan any individual site on the spot for an
instant verdict. It's the raw material everything else is built on: the system can only protect
you from the places it knows you go."

---

## SECTION 5 · SECURITY OPERATIONS

### 14 · Alerts & Evidence  `[DEMO: open an alert, show its detail + the evidence locker]`

**VO:** "Alerts and Evidence is your triage desk. When something crosses the danger line, it
lands in the alert queue here, ranked by severity. Click any alert to see its full detail — and
everything connected to it, the proof, is collected in the evidence locker beside it. It's how a
single warning becomes a complete, organised case you can actually act on."

### 15 · Investigations  `[DEMO: switch between ACTIVE and CLOSED cases]`

**VO:** "Investigations is where those cases are managed. Related alerts and evidence are grouped
into a case you can work through from start to finish — active investigations on one tab, closed
ones on the other. It brings order to a messy situation, so nothing slips through the cracks and
you always know exactly where each threat stands."

### 16 · Incident Timeline  `[DEMO: scrub the event timeline for an incident]`

**VO:** "Incident Timeline lays everything out in order, on a clock. For any incident, you can
see exactly what happened and when — the full sequence from first sighting to final resolution.
When you need to understand the story of an attack, or explain it to someone else, this is the
page that tells it, minute by minute."

---

## SECTION 6 · REPORTS

### 17 · Reports  `[DEMO: click Generate → pick a period → watch it write → Export PDF]`

**VO:** "And finally, Reports — where everything comes together into something you can keep and
share. I click Generate, choose a period — the last eight hours, a day, a week, or a month — and
CyberForge reads its own memory and your browsing history for that window, then writes a full,
detailed security report: what you did, what it found, what it dealt with, and what it recommends
next. The report is written by the AI, it's saved to your library, and you can export it as a
clean, branded PDF. From raw activity to a professional report, in a single click."

**VO:** "That's CyberForge, end to end — from a live threat dashboard, through an autonomous AI
agent and a memory that learns, to reports that write themselves. Corporate‑grade security,
powered by AI, running entirely on your own device — where your data stays private. CyberForge:
security that thinks, so you don't have to worry."
